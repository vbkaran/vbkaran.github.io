Recent Research & AI

My recent work brings machine learning into the empirical economist’s toolkit — combining the causal-inference designs I’ve long relied on (regression discontinuity, difference-in-differences, instrumental variables) with predictive and AI-driven methods. The aim is the same as ever: cleaner identification, richer measurement, and evidence policymakers can act on.

Two threads sit closest to this today. First, inflation targeting under supply shocks — using large language models to read three decades of Reserve Bank of India communication, alongside my earlier Phillips-curve and inflation-modelling work. Second, nowcasting: Bayesian machine-learning models that turn high-frequency digital-infrastructure data (GST, UPI, credit) into real-time reads on the economy.
